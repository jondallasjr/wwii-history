WWII Timeline

Major Events 1939-1945

1939

September 1, 1939

Germany Invades Poland

Germany launches Operation Fall Weiß, invading Poland. Britain and France declare war on Germany two days later, officially beginning World War II in Europe.

Historic Significance: Start of WWII in Europe
1940

May-June 1940

Fall of France

Germany defeats France in the Battle of France. The French sign an armistice on June 22, 1940. Britain stands alone against Nazi Germany.

Historic Significance: Shift in European power dynamics
1940

July-October 1940

Battle of Britain

Germany's Luftwaffe attempts to establish air superiority over Britain as a prelude to invasion. The RAF's successful defense becomes a turning point in the war.

Historic Significance: First major defeat of Nazi Germany
1941

June 22, 1941

Operation Barbarossa

Germany invades the Soviet Union, breaking the Nazi-Soviet Non-Aggression Pact. This opens the largest theater of war in human history.

Historic Significance: Soviet Union enters the war; massive scale conflict begins
1941

December 7, 1941

Attack on Pearl Harbor

Japan attacks the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii. The United States declares war on Japan the next day, entering WWII.

Historic Significance: United States enters the war
1942

August-February 1942-43

Battle of Stalingrad

One of the bloodiest battles in human history. Soviet forces under General Zhukov defeat the German 6th Army, marking a major turning point on the Eastern Front.

Historic Significance: First major German defeat; Soviet momentum begins
1942

June 4-7, 1942

Battle of Midway

U.S. naval forces defeat the Japanese fleet in the Pacific. This marks the beginning of American naval superiority in the Pacific Theater.

Historic Significance: Turning point in the Pacific War
1943

July 10, 1943

Invasion of Sicily

Operation Husky: Allied forces invade Sicily, establishing a foothold in Europe. This leads to the invasion of mainland Italy.

Historic Significance: Beginning of Allied invasion of Europe
1944

June 6, 1944

D-Day: Normandy Invasion

Operation Overlord: Largest amphibious invasion in history. Over 150,000 Allied troops land on the beaches of Normandy, establishing a western front in Europe.

Historic Significance: Beginning of liberation of Western Europe
1944

August-September 1944

Liberation of Paris

French and American forces liberate Paris from German occupation. General de Gaulle leads the ceremonial march down the Champs-Élysées.

Historic Significance: Symbolic restoration of France
1945

January-May 1945

Soviet Advance to Berlin

Soviet forces advance through Eastern Europe toward Berlin. The Red Army captures Warsaw and pushes toward Germany's capital.

Historic Significance: Soviet dominance in Eastern Europe
1945

April 30, 1945

Hitler's Suicide

Adolf Hitler commits suicide in his bunker as Soviet forces close in. He designates Admiral Karl Dönitz as his successor.

Historic Significance: End of Nazi leadership
1945

May 7-8, 1945

Germany Surrenders

Germany signs the Instrument of Surrender, ending the war in Europe. May 8 is celebrated as Victory in Europe Day (V-E Day).

Historic Significance: End of war in European Theater
1945

August 6 & 9, 1945

Atomic Bombs on Japan

The United States drops atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Japan surrenders on August 15, 1945.

Historic Significance: End of war in Pacific Theater; introduction of nuclear weapons
1945

September 2, 1945

V-J Day: Official Japanese Surrender

Japan formally signs the Instrument of Surrender aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay. World War II officially ends.

Historic Significance: Official end of WWII

This timeline presents the major events of World War II from 1939 to 1945. For more detailed information, explore our Major Battles, Key Figures, and Resources sections.
