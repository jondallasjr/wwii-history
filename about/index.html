About This Site

WWII History is a comprehensive, educational resource dedicated to providing accurate, well-researched information about World War II.

Our Mission

We are committed to preserving and sharing the history of World War II for educators, students, historians, and the general public. Our mission is to make this pivotal period of human history accessible, understandable, and relevant to contemporary audiences while maintaining the highest standards of historical accuracy.

Accuracy Standards

Every article, timeline entry, and resource on this site undergoes rigorous fact-checking and review. We:

  • Consult primary historical documents and reputable academic sources
  • Cross-reference information across multiple authoritative sources
  • Cite our sources transparently throughout all content
  • Correct errors promptly when identified
  • Maintain editorial standards aligned with academic historical research
  • Present multiple perspectives on contested historical interpretations

Our Sources

This site draws from a diverse range of scholarly and primary sources, including:

  • Academic histories and peer-reviewed journals
  • Government archives and official documents
  • Museum collections and institutional research
  • Eyewitness accounts and oral histories
  • Military records and strategic analyses
  • Contemporary newspapers and publications

Content Coverage

WWII History covers the major events, figures, and themes of World War II, including:

  • Timeline: Major events from 1939-1945 and their historical context
  • Battles: Detailed accounts of significant military engagements
  • Key Figures: Biographies of important political, military, and civilian leaders
  • Theaters of War: European and Pacific theaters with regional context
  • Technology: Military innovations and technological advances
  • The Holocaust: Comprehensive coverage of this pivotal and tragic history
  • Home Front: Civilian experiences and societal impacts of the war

Educational Philosophy

We believe historical education should be comprehensive, balanced, and thought-provoking. Our content is designed to inform readers about what happened, why it happened, and how it continues to shape our world today. We encourage critical thinking and welcome questions about historical interpretation.

Get in Touch

Have questions or suggestions? We'd love to hear from you. Please visit our contact page to reach out, or explore our resources section for additional learning materials.
